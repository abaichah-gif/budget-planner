Without Universal Credit, Child Benefit, or Council Tax Reduction, she has zero fallback if something goes wrong. The good news: £2,000/month is workable in Northampton — but only with a strict system. Building an emergency fund isn't optional, it's survival.

Worth checking: future eligibility & Council Tax discount

🛒 Grocery Strategy: £40/week for Two
Tight but completely doable with smart shopping

🏪 Shop at Aldi on Towcester Road

Aldi is consistently the cheapest UK supermarket. A weekly shop of basics for a mum and 4-year-old runs £25–40 there. Do the main shop Saturday morning with a list, then only grab milk/bread mid-week. Never do a "full shop" at Tesco or Sainsbury's — it'll cost 30–40% more for the same items.

📱 Download Too Good To Go — save £30–50/month

Surprise Bags from Greggs, Costa, Morrisons, and local bakeries cost £2–4 and contain £8–15 worth of food. Grab one 2–3 times a week for snacks, pastries, and sometimes full meals. This single app can cut her food spend by 20%.

🟡 Yellow sticker runs — 75% off meat and ready meals

Tesco and Morrisons in Northampton start big markdowns after 7pm. Meat, fish, bread, and ready meals go to 25p–£1. Buy and freeze immediately. Many families build entire freezers this way.

🍲 Batch cook Sundays — save time AND money

Cook 3–4 big meals (chilli, pasta bake, curry, stew) and freeze in portions. A slow cooker from Argos (£15–20) pays for itself in weeks by replacing takeaways. Uses 80% less energy than an oven too.

📱 Phone → SMARTY: save £7/month

Unlimited data for ~£8/month, no contract, rolling monthly. Uses Three's network. If she barely uses data, Lebara does 5GB for just £5/month.

📡 Broadband: negotiate or switch — save £5–10/month

Call the provider and say she's leaving. They'll offer a deal. NOW Broadband starts at £18/month. Some providers offer social tariffs based on low income (not just benefits).

⚡ Energy: smart habits — save £15–25/month

Ofgem's price cap drops 7% from April 2026. Beyond that: wash at 30°C, air-dry clothes, use a slow cooker instead of oven, LED bulbs, draught tape on windows (£3 from Wilko). A heated throw blanket (£25 from Argos) costs ~3p/hour vs 30p+ for gas heating.

🏛️ Council Tax: 25% single person discount — save £29/month

This is often available regardless of immigration status — it's based on who lives in the property, not benefit eligibility. Her 4-year-old doesn't count. Apply to West Northamptonshire Council. Worst case they say no.

👦 Free & Cheap Things for Her Son
Being on a budget doesn't mean missing out

🌳 Free outdoor days

Abington Park — huge park with playground, lake, and free museum. Becket's Park by the river. Delapré Abbey gardens. Brixworth Country Park — woodland trails (short bus ride).

📚 Free activities

Northampton Central Library — free story time and Saturday children's activities. Northampton Museum — free entry. Check Facebook local groups for free toddler events and playgroups around town.

📺 Free entertainment at home

BBC iPlayer, ITVX, Channel 4 streaming, Pluto TV — all free. YouTube Kids is perfect for a 4-year-old. She doesn't need Netflix.

👕 Clothes & toys: second-hand first

Vinted and Facebook Marketplace for kids' clothes (barely worn, fraction of the price). Charity shops on Kettering Road for toys and books (50p–£2). OLIO app and Northampton Freecycle for free items from neighbours.
